1. Introduction

Hello everyone, my name is Alfaiz Alafi Luthfie, a UI/UX Designer and front end web developer. Here I will share the results of my design work related to the case study "Citra Katering (Citra)".

2. Studi Kasus

Greater Jakarta has great potential in the type of business providing food and drinks which is equipped with equipment and supplies for the manufacturing, storage and serving process, then served at the location desired by the customer or what is known as catering services. The catering service business has a variety of needs, including catering services for office events, home events, wedding catering, rice boxes, coffee breaks, snack boxes and so on. The offline catering service business process has many obstacles such as fraud, unsatisfactory service and inefficiency. For this reason, I created an application design "Citra Catering Online" which can solve this problem.

3. Executive Summary

In this project, a system will be designed, namely an online food catering ordering application, which is an information system that aims to increase efficiency and effectiveness in the food catering ordering process, especially in the Jakarta area and surrounding cities.

Work on this information system project was carried out in accordance with the System Development Life Cycle (SDLC) methodology, namely the Agile methodology which is a software development approach that is flexible and adaptable and is incrementally iterative. With this method, projects are made more flexible to changing needs and users can see project results earlier during the development process

4. Design Goals

The purpose of making the design is as follows :

  • Aims to create an online application for ordering food catering in the Jakarta City area and its surroundings
  • Designed to make it easier for users to order food catering
  • Providing a platform and forum for merchants to sell their food
  • Improve the efficiency of the user experience for ordering catering online.

7. Wireframe

A wireframe is a simple visual representation of a user interface or web page depicted in the form of lines, basic geometric shapes, and text. Wireframes usually don't have colors, images, or other design elements. It aims to highlight the structure and layout of the main elements of an interface without the distraction of visual design.

8. High Fidelity

In the context of user interface (UI) and user experience (UX) design, high fidelity refers to a prototype or design that approximates or accurately reflects the actual appearance and interactions of the final product. The results of high fidelity are:

There is an onboarding display that briefly explains the application. Users can log in with Google, and there is a home display.

There is a filter page that is used to filter food according to the user's tastes and needs. There is a food menu along with a description when the user wants to use catering services in this application.

There is a display of search results and also notifications when the user makes a purchase. For payments, Full Payment and Downpayment features are provided, and there are also many payment options available to make things easier for users.

There is also a special merchant view that is used to manage food catering sales and can add and delete food menus. There is also a notification when an order is placed by a user or consumer.
